Snowy weather to stick around until Monday morning rush hour

If you thought those messy winter commutes into work were bad memories of the past, think again. Several bursts of flurries left nearly 2 centimetres of wet snow on the ground in the city Saturday afternoon, but Environment Canada says the worst of the weekend is still to come. According to the agency, there’s a 70 per cent chance of more snow overnight as the mercury dips to a frigid -7 C. Things will clear up by Sunday morning but the freezing temperatures – a high of -1 C and a low of -6 C during the day – mean the snow will be back even stronger in the afternoon and into the night, with an expected 5-10 cm of snow. The snow should finally stop just in time for the rush hour around 8 a.m. Monday morning. When it’s all said and done, conditions for commuters will still be messy and slick. While snowy days in April aren’t exactly uncommon, it’s been a little while since Toronto’s seen similar amounts of powder in a single event during the first full month of spring. You would have to think all the way back to April 2-3, 2005 when 16 cm of snow hit the tarmac at Pearson airport over a two day storm. The snowiest April on record is all the way back in 1939 when 32 cm fell in total after a whopping 26 cm fell on a single day. On average, only 6 cm of snow falls throughout the entire month. At this time of year, temperatures usually hover around a high of 9 C and a low of 1 C. It’s not all bad news. “It should end up being a warmer than usual spring for Toronto despite the rough start,” says Environment Canada climatologist Dave Phillips. “But April can definitely be a cruel month.”
